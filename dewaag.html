Summary of De Waag

De Waag talked about different layers in what the user sees on their phones and what the reality is in what is underneath. There is a lot underneath like the stack, design process and fundament.

Next thing they talked about is mobile apps and the privacy of it. They asked question about how important privacy is for us and if we ever think about it. They gave us a few exercises where we express how important privacy is to us and describe our ideal phones.

Conclusion: what I learned

Privacy is not something i want to worry about too much, because i really believe my data is probably already out there. Even if i try to do my best to keep my own data save i believe it can never be 100% save as long i'm online. I have turned on my 2fa on practically every account i own but by the end of the day the companies that hold my data are they ones responsible for keeping my data save. And they don't always do a very good job at it. I've been in a few data breaches where my data got leaked and there is nothing i can do about it. I do believe that companies need to take privacy more seriously and that if they ask for your data they have to take the responsibility that comes with it.
