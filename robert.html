About Robert Spier

Robert is a creative technologist at Deloitte NL. He used to be a lecturer at HVA and worked at Booreiland(now clarify).

Summary of Robert Spier's lecture

Today's lecture is was all about giving us advice on life after cmd. How to find a job, where and how much money to ask for.

Us

He starts by talking about WHERE you might want to work. Design agency, start up, webshop, freelance, corporate or something else. Then he continues talking about WHAT you want to do. Write code all day, talk with clients/stakeholders, Do (user) research, experiment with new tech, manage stuff or do something totally different. HOW and WHERE to get this job: stay at your internship, go for job hunt, find yourself somewhere exotic, continue studying, traineeship or have no clue. Each of these have different advantages and disadvantages. Robert has asked different CMD alumni about there current jobs and how much money they earn. They all do different things and earn between 2000 and 3000 euro's. He gave as advice learn the fundamentals, to always remain curious, stay informed and enjoy discovering. It's best to ask each other to make better decisions. You can learn everything once you know the basics. You can decide to work at different places and they all have their own advantages and disadvantages. But the important thing is that you always talk to each other about these things.

Money vs benefits

There is money and there are benefits. You have to weight them together to find a good balance. part-time you have to get the bruto and multiply by 0.8. Another important thing is you should calculate how much your own spendings are each month. That way you have a better estimate on how much money you need to ask for. The money should be realistic and the expectations for a junior too.

Conclusion: what I learned

These are good and handy tips to remember. they are relevant to me as a beginner who will soon go out and find my way into a job that i have to like. Knowing the practical side of these things will hopefully help me find a job i like, earn enough and explore better to all the possibilities.
