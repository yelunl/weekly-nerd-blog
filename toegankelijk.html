About Marieke de Hoop en Tim Teuwisse

Marieke de Hoop (ex-cmd'er) and Tim Teuwisse are both working for the company Digitaal Toegankelijk. Here they test website of other companies and measure how much they are accessible. They give advice on how to make their sites more accessible.

Summary of Digitaal Toegankelijk

The big 4: Perceivable, operable, understandable and robust.

Today talk was all about web accessibility. They talked about the big 4 of the WCAG:

  • Perceivable

    Can this content be viewed by anyone?

  • Operable

    Can this content be reached and used by everyone?

  • Understandable

    Can this content be understood by everyone?

  • Robust

    Is the technology behind my digital product semantically correct?

These are the main standards of creating an accessible website according to WCAG. And they should be tested on these 4 points. According to them there are a few criteria that help to get all 4 points.

  • 1.4.2 - Audio Control

    If the sound takes longer than 3 seconds, there must be an option to pause/stop it or reduce the volume.

  • 2.1.2 - No keyboard traps

    When you navigate with the keyboard, it should not happen that you can no longer navigate out of an element or part of the website.

  • 2.2.2 - Pause, stop, hide

    All moving, flashing, scrolling or updating elements that: start automatically, last longer than 5 seconds and is shown parallel to other content must be able to paused, stopped or hidden.

  • 2.3.2 - Three flashes or below threshold

    On web pages no element should flash 3 or more times in a second.

Another advice they would give is on how to test our own website for accessibility:

  • Use a screen reader

    This way we can we certain that our site it accessible for people with screen readers.

  • Navigate your site with a keyboard (without a mouse)

    To make sure there are not keyboard traps, you can use the tab key to navigate through a website.

  • Test with the target audience

    They do really want to help you

Conclusion: what I learned

The importance of accessibility of a website is becoming more and more important. There is a law in the EU that says that by 2025 all websites must be accessible for everyone and if they are not there could be a fine given to companies. Other things I learned are the Big 4 and advice on how to reach those criteria and making my own websites accessible.
